\subsection{spacetime}

The $d$ dimensionnal Lorentzian manifold will be then denoted by $(\Mcal,\gsf)$. To conserve the causality the manifold is assumed to be globally hyperbolic, it means it admits spacelike Cauchy hypersurfaces, furthermore we will assume $\Mcal$ to be a Hausdorff, connected, and smooth. manifold on $\Mcal$, endowed with a Lorentzian metric $\gsf$. We also require that $\Mcal$ has to be second countable, or equivalently paracompact, i.e. its topology has a countable basis, and orientable and time-orientable.
